EVERYDAY LANGUAGE

Recognising and interpreting the signs that are most frequently used in everyday communication, makes communication easier for people who have speaking or hearing impairment, especially in situations where they need to communicate with people who don't know sign language. By recognizing and interpreting the signs for common words and phrases, the assistant can help facilitate communication between different groups of people.Here are some of them that you may use.

Hello

HELLO

Place the hand you are writing with on your forehead close to your ear and move it outwards and away from your body.

Yes

YES

Take a hand and make it into a fist and bob it back and forth, resembling a head nodding

No

NO

Take your first two fingers and tap them with your thumb, resembling a mouth saying no.

Please

PLEASE

Placing your dominant hand with your thumb sticking out and fingers extended over your chest. Then, move your hand in circular motion (clockwise) two or three times.

Thankyou

THANKYOU

Start with your dominant hand. Then place your fingers near your lips, with your hand as flat as possible. Move your hand forward and slightly downwards toward the person you are thanking.

Cat

CAT

Pinch your thumb and index finger together by the side of each cheek, while keeping your remaining fingers straight out.

Me

ME

Point the index finger towards yourself

What

WHAT

Turn up your open hands, with fingers spread apart and hands slightly curved and relaxed.

Food

Food

Squeeze your dominant hand and move it to your mouth as if you would put a piece of food in your mouth.

Water

WATER

Take your dominant hand and extend and separate the three middle fingers while holding the thumb and pinkie finger together. Then tap your index finger on your chin a couple of times.

Need

NEED

Start with an "X"-handshape (the fingerspelled letter "X") and then bend it downward from the wrist.

Why

WHY

Wiggle the middle finger a couple of times

Phone

PHONE

Stick out your pinkie and thumb for a “Y.”Press your other fingers against your palm.

You

YOU

Point with your finger at the person you are referring to.

Your

YOUR

Aim your palm at the other person.

I Love You

I LOVE YOU

Point out your thumb and index finger to form an “L”. While keeping them extended, lift your little finger. Your middle and ring finger keep touching your palm.
