AMERICAN SIGN
LANGUAGE


American Sign Language (ASL) is a visual-gestural language used by most of the dump and deaf community.With signing, the brain processes linguistic information through the eyes. ASL beginners usually start with learning the alphabet. It is a complete, natural language that has the same linguistic properties as spoken languages.Like any spoken language, ASL is a language with its own unique rules of grammar and syntax.The gestures or symbols in sign language are organized in a linguistic way.

SIGN-I-FY

ALPHABETS

A

A

Make a fist with your thumb pointing up to form an “A.”

Hold your hand in front of you with your palm facing forward. Close your fingers in a fist, but keep your thumb pressed against the side of your hand and pointing upwards.

B

B

Hold your fingers together with your thumb across your palm for “B.”

Extend your fingers so they are straight. Bring them in close to each other so they are touching on the sides. Then, fold your thumb across the front of your palm and hold it there.

C

C

Curl your whole hand into the shape of a "C"

Turn your right hand so that your palm is facing towards the left. Then, curl your fingers and thumb to form a half circle or backward “C.” Hold your hand still.

D

D

Touch your fingertips to your thumb and point to make a “D”

Touch the tip of your thumb to every finger except your index finger. Then, point your index finger straight up. Hold your hand still with your palm facing forward.

E

E

Form the letter “B” and curl your fingertips down to make an “E.”

Bend all four fingers down to meet your thumb. Press the tips of your fingers against the side of your thumb.

F

F

Press your index finger and thumb together with straight fingers for “F.”

Keep your other 3 fingers straight.

G

G

Point your index finger and turn your hand to the side to make a “G.”

Make a fist and extend your index finger and rest your thumb against your middle finger.Your palm should face you and your index finger to your left.

H

H

Stick out your middle and index fingers to form an “H.”

Keep your hand in the same position as it was for the letter “G,” but extend your middle finger as well.The palm should face you and your index and middle fingers to your left.

I

I

Point your pinkie straight up to make an “I.”

Form a fist and poke your pinkie finger straight up.

J

J

Use your pinkie to draw the shape of a "J".

With your hand still in the position for the letter “I,” bring your finger down and then swoop it upwards

K

K

Point the index and middle fingers with a thumb between them for “K.”

Press the ring finger and pinkie finger against the palm.Hold your index and middle finger straight up.Then,press thumb against your palm,between your index and middle finger

L

L

Form an L-shape with your thumb and index finger to make an “L.”

Press your middle, ring, and pinkie fingers against your palm as you form an L-shape with your index finger and thumb.

M

M

Hold an invisible ball and poke your thumb through for "M."

Curl your fingers down towards your palm with your thumb between your index and middle finger.Then, poke your thumb between your ring and pinky finger.

N

N

Poke your thumb between your middle and ring finger for “N.”

Curl your fingers as if they’re holding a ball. Then, push the tip of your thumb between your middle and ring fingers.

O

O

Make an "O" with your fingers and thumb.

Press the tips of your fingers and thumb together.

P

P

Press your thumb against your middle finger with index pointed for “P.”

Press your ring finger and pinkie finger against your palm. Then, extend your index finger. Extend your middle finger so it's pointing down and touch it with your thumb.

Q

Q

Make the sign for a “G” and then point down for a “Q.”

Press your middle, ring, and pinkie fingers against your palm and point with your index finger. Then, rest your thumb on top of your middle finger. Then, point down with your hand.

R

R

Cross your index finger over your middle finger for an “R.”

Press your ring finger, pinkie, and thumb against your palm. Then, wrap your middle finger around the back of your index finger.

S

S

Make a fist and put your thumb on top of your fingers for an “S.”

This is often confused with "A" so pay careful attention to the thumb position. Press your fingers against your palm and wrap your thumb over them.

T

T

Form a fist with your thumb between the middle and index finger for “T.”

Press your fingers against your palm. Then, push your thumb up between your index and middle finger.

U

U

Point your middle and index fingers upwards together to make “U.”

Press your thumb, ring finger, and pinkie finger against your palm. Then, point your middle and index finger straight up and hold them against each other.

V

V

Make a "U" and separate your fingers for a “V.”

With your hand in the “U” position, spread your fingers apart.Make sure to keep your ring finger, pinkie, and thumb pressed against your palm

W

W

Hold up 3 fingers and spread them apart to form “W.”

Press your pinkie finger to your palm using your thumb. Then, hold up your index, middle, and ring fingers and spread them apart

X

X

Make a fist, then raise and crook your index finger to make “X.”

Press your thumb against your palm and then wrap your middle, ring, and pinkie fingers over it. Curl your index finger.

Y

Y

Stick out your pinkie and thumb for a “Y.”

Press your other fingers against your palm.

Z

Z

Write the letter “Z” in the air with your index finger.

Form the letter "D", with your index finger out, and write a letter “Z” in the air
